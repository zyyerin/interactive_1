Chuunibyou

By Erin Zhang, this is serious

This article is about Chuunibyou as a concept. For Chuunibyou as a disease, see Chuunibyou influenza. For the 2020 outbreak, see 2020 Chuuni pandemic.

Chuunibyou(中二病) is a Japanese term which roughly translates to Middle School 2nd Year Syndrome. It is a ridiculous and painful thought or such a behavior those are frequently seen among 14-year-old teenagers. In general, people with Chuunibyou try to surpass anybody else. Hikaru Ijuin is considered to be the person who first used this term. There are 3 main types of Chuunibyou: DQN, Subculture, and
Evil Eye. Typical Chuunibyou symptoms include but are not limited to strong imagination [or VERY STRONG IMAGINATION], obsession with Mythology terms, interest in darkness, belief in possession of supernatural powers. Usually, Chuunibyou can be cured through pass of time. Yet most people suffered from serious sequels from time to time [find more]. Though Chuunibyou is common, it's okay if you don't understand it. In the end it's their problem.
